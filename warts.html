What are the symptoms, causes, treatments for warts?



         19 March, 2021                  Blog               0      



A wart is a small growth that appears on the surface of a person’s skin. They are not cancerous or harmful, but they have an unsightly appearance. The HPV is a type of virus that causes common warts. The virus usually gets in through a cut or a scratch, that causes the infection. The infection generates the skin cells to produce rapidly, which causes the wart to form. HPV can be attained by touching an infected person or object.

The symptoms of wart are the presence of a small, grainy growth on the skin. Commonly warts occur on hands and fingers. Warts possibly will appear the same color as a person’s skin. The Wart will be caused by the clotting of blood vessels.

Warts can be caused by a virus called the human papillomavirus, People mostly contract it via a scrape or cut in the skin. People who bite their fingernails are more likely to contract HPV, as well as those who pick at the skin around their fingernails. Children and teens are at an increased risk due to scratches or cuts.

Warts tend to take months to grow large enough to be noticed. It is difficult to know exactly that when and why the wart has been caused. The treatment for warts can be done, without any type of treatment, common warts will normally go away on their own. However, it can take up to 18 months to disappear. Treatment includes freezing spray and salicylic acid if this treatment is not effective then visit for professional treatment to the dermatologist. Treatment includes cantharidin, cryotherapy, curettage, and electro surgery. If necessary, a dermatologist can remove the wart by cutting it out of the skin. Prevention of wart can be done, If the person already has wart should avoid picking or scratching at it if not it could spread to different areas of the body. If someone is having wart avoid it. Wear latex gloves and wash your hands after touching the wart. Keep the wart dry, as much as possible.
